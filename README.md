# COLOR CRAZE RULES

Color Craze is quite complex compared to regular Uno. The changes are as follows:

## Color Loop

There are eight main colors in Color Craze: Red, Orange, Yellow, Green, Cyan (what used to be blue), Blue, Purple, and Magenta. These colors are connected in a loop. On your turn, rather than matching the exact color, you can play a card of the previous or next color in that loop.

## Double Colors

There are four colors which each count as two of the main colors, indicated by displaying both colors on the card. They are: Red-Cyan, Orange-Blue, Yellow-Purple, and Green-Magenta. When playing this card (or playing on it), you may use it as either of its two colors.

## White Cards

White cards are unique. They have no numbers, only the symbols of the normal cards. They can be played on any color, similarly to a Wild Card. Unlike a Wild Card, however, you cannot choose what color continues play; instead, the next player may play whichever color they choose on it. It is a double-edged sword; if it is the last card in your hand, then your victory is nearly guaranteed, but otherwise, if the next player in turn has Uno themselves, then it'd be better to hold onto them.

## Grey Cards

Grey cards are more accurately described as 'colorless'. They have only numbered variants and cannot be played on any color, including themselves; nor can any color be played on them besides Wild, meaning you have to match the symbol. If you have this card, it'd be best to play it the moment you get the opportunity.
